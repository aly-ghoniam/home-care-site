# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Families (adult children aged 35–65) researching care options for elderly parents in London. Secondary: the elderly individuals themselves (70–90), who may visit directly but are usually represented by a family decision-maker. Users arrive stressed, time-poor, and emotionally loaded — they need trust signals immediately, clear contact details above the fold, and friction-free ways to pick up the phone.

## Product Purpose

Marketing website for a London-based domiciliary home care company. The single goal is to convert a visitor into a phone call. No forms, no online booking, no map embeds — just credibility, warmth, and a prominent phone number on every page. Success means a visitor picks up the phone before they scroll past the fold.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, warm, capable. Not clinical. Not corporate. The brand should feel like a knowledgeable friend who happens to run a care company — reassuring without being saccharine, professional without being cold.

## Anti-references

- Generic NHS-blue health portal aesthetic (institutional, cold)
- SaaS startup landing pages (dark mesh + centered hero, AI-purple accents, Notion-style minimalism)
- Retirement home brochure clichés (stock photo of smiling elderly woman with caregiver, soft-focus lavender palette)
- Any design that makes a stressed family member feel like they're filling out a form or navigating a funnel

## Design Principles

1. **Phone first.** The phone number is always visible. Every CTA resolves to a `tel:` link. The number must be readable without glasses.
2. **Trust before conversion.** Credibility signals (CQC registration, years of experience, real testimonials) come before any call-to-action pressure.
3. **Calm over clever.** No visual tricks, no motion that competes with content. Animations are background, not foreground.
4. **Elderly-legible at every breakpoint.** Minimum 18px body text. 4.5:1 contrast everywhere, including placeholder states. Mobile layout tested at 375px.
5. **Placeholder integrity.** All unconfirmed client content is visibly marked `[PLACEHOLDER]` — the site must never look finished when it isn't.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Elderly primary audience: body text ≥18px, contrast ≥4.5:1 on all surfaces including tinted backgrounds. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on every animation. No reliance on color alone to convey meaning. Semantic HTML throughout (landmark regions, heading hierarchy).
